Patented Innovation

AI Noise Cancellation for Industrial Environments

Purpose-built audio processing that suppresses shop noise before AI speech-to-text and analysis. Not a generic noise gate — a system trained on the specific acoustic profiles of service and industrial environments.

The Problem

A technician is narrating their inspection findings while standing next to a running engine. An impact wrench fires at 100dB three feet away. A pneumatic compressor cycles on. The shop PA system announces a call on line two.

Generic noise cancellation — the kind built for Zoom calls and call centers — was trained on office noise: keyboard clatter, HVAC hum, distant conversation. It doesn't know what an impact wrench sounds like. It doesn't know how to separate a compressor cycle from human speech in the same frequency range. It fails.

When noise cancellation fails, speech-to-text accuracy collapses. When transcription fails, AI analysis produces garbage. The entire value chain breaks at the audio layer.

TruVideo's Approach

Our patented noise cancellation is trained specifically on automotive, fleet, aviation, field service, insurance, and construction audio profiles. The models understand the spectral characteristics of impact wrenches, compressors, lifts, engine sounds, grinders, rivet guns, and dozens of other industrial noise sources.

This isn't noise suppression — it's noise-aware audio processing that preserves speech fidelity while removing environmental interference. The result: 30%+ improvement in downstream AI model accuracy compared to generic noise cancellation or no processing.

This matters because every AI capability that depends on audio — transcription, sentiment analysis, finding extraction, compliance verification — only works if the audio is clean enough for accurate processing.

Patented Innovation

Spatial Damage Detection from Mobile Video

AI computer vision that watches a technician's walkaround video and automatically detects, classifies, and spatially localizes damage — then maps it to specific vehicle zones with severity estimates.

1

Detect

Identifies damage in real-world video frames: scratches, dents, cracks, corrosion, fluid leaks, tire wear, paint defects, glass damage. Works with shaky handheld footage, poor lighting, and partial occlusion — the conditions that exist in every real service environment.

2

Classify

Categorizes each finding by type (scratch, dent, crack, corrosion, wear), severity (minor, moderate, severe), and affected component (specific body panel, wheel, glass, undercarriage). This isn't generic object detection — it requires vehicle-specific understanding of body panels, components, and damage patterns.

3

Localize

Maps each finding to a spatial zone on the vehicle. Not just "front bumper" — specific coordinates within a spatial model. Tracks damage progression over time across multiple inspections. Creates structured, queryable records that feed downstream systems for estimates, claims, and compliance.

Why Generic Object Detection Fails

Off-the-shelf computer vision models (YOLO, Detectron, foundation models) are trained on general-purpose datasets. They can identify "a car" in an image. They cannot identify a 2cm scratch on the right rear quarter panel of a 2023 Honda Accord filmed from a shaky phone camera in a dimly lit service bay.

That level of specificity requires domain-specific training data (which TruVideo has 80M+ videos of), vehicle-specific spatial models (which require understanding of make/model body geometry), and inference pipelines optimized for mobile video input (which require years of engineering for edge cases like motion blur, exposure changes, and partial visibility).
