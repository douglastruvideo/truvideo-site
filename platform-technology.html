Patented Innovation

AI Noise Cancellation for Industrial Environments

Purpose-built audio processing that suppresses shop noise before AI speech-to-text and analysis. Not a generic noise gate — a system trained on the specific acoustic profiles of service and industrial environments.

The Problem

A technician is narrating their inspection findings while standing next to a running engine. An impact wrench fires at 100dB three feet away. A pneumatic compressor cycles on. The shop PA system announces a call on line two.

Generic noise cancellation — the kind built for Zoom calls and call centers — was trained on office noise: keyboard clatter, HVAC hum, distant conversation. It doesn't know what an impact wrench sounds like. It doesn't know how to separate a compressor cycle from human speech in the same frequency range. It fails.

When noise cancellation fails, speech-to-text accuracy collapses. When transcription fails, AI analysis produces garbage. The entire value chain breaks at the audio layer.

TruVideo's Approach

Our patented noise cancellation is trained specifically on automotive, fleet, aviation, field service, insurance, and construction audio profiles. The models understand the spectral characteristics of impact wrenches, compressors, lifts, engine sounds, grinders, rivet guns, and dozens of other industrial noise sources.

This isn't noise suppression — it's noise-aware audio processing that preserves speech fidelity while removing environmental interference. The result: 30%+ improvement in downstream AI model accuracy compared to generic noise cancellation or no processing.

This matters because every AI capability that depends on audio — transcription, sentiment analysis, finding extraction, compliance verification — only works if the audio is clean enough for accurate processing.

The Spatial Inspection Platform

Far more than damage detection. TruVideo's patent-pending Spatial Inspection Platform covers four families of innovations that together create a complete system for inspecting, measuring, and tracking physical objects over time — from a smartphone.

I

Anchor-Referenced Spatial Annotation

Patent Pending

Persistent spatial annotations that stay anchored to physical objects using AR and feature-anchored coordinate systems. An annotation placed on a dent, a crack, or any finding stays locked to that exact location — across sessions, across devices, across inspectors. Automatic scale calibration means measurements are real-world accurate without manual setup. Annotations are encoded in a device-independent, viewing-angle-independent format so any inspector on any device sees the same spatial data.

II

Passive Crowdsourced Geometry Acquisition

Patent Pending

Building 3D geometry databases as a passive byproduct of routine inspections. No dedicated scanning sessions. No specialized hardware. No manufacturer CAD data needed. Every inspection contributes to increasingly accurate 3D models that improve future inspections. Multiple independent inspection events refine the same models over time, creating a compounding data asset: more inspections produce better models, and better models produce better inspections.

III

Shadow-Based Dimensional Measurement

Patent Pending

Measuring real physical dimensions from a smartphone photo using shadow geometry and the device flash as a controlled illumination source with a known spatial offset. This is trigonometric computation from known physical parameters — not neural network depth estimation, not LiDAR, not structured light. Applications include tire tread depth, crack width, gap distance, and surface relief measurement, all from a standard smartphone camera.

IV

Temporal Anomaly Detection

Patent Pending

Baseline-to-change detection on spatially registered 3D surfaces. The system automatically detects what changed between inspections, classifies changes by type and severity, and performs multi-temporal progressive analysis — tracking rate of change and predicting future condition. Applications span vehicle damage tracking, facility management, and aviation maintenance where understanding how things change over time is as important as detecting what exists today.

Why These Four Capabilities Together Matter

Each claim family is independently valuable. Together, they create a closed loop: spatial annotations (I) persist across time, crowdsourced geometry (II) builds the 3D models those annotations attach to, shadow measurement (III) provides real-world dimensional data without specialized hardware, and temporal detection (IV) tracks how annotated findings evolve. No existing solution combines all four.

This is the foundation for TruVideo Inspect, FacilityEye, and every vertical-specific product we build — a universal spatial inspection platform that works on any physical object, from any smartphone, with no dedicated scanning and no specialized equipment.
