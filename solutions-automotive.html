Build It Yourself?

Here's what your team would actually have to solve.

Video from a service bay is not video from a studio. It's shot one-handed on a phone with a cracked screen over spotty WiFi in a noisy shop. Every one of these problems is a production incident waiting to happen.

Mobile capture on bad networks

Service bays have dead zones. Technicians walk from WiFi to 4G mid-recording. Without chunked resumable uploads, a 90-second walkaround video fails at 95% and the technician has to re-record. Your support queue fills up with "video won't upload."

Upload failure is the #1 support complaint for in-house video

Device fragmentation

Thousands of Android device models with different camera APIs, OEM modifications, OS versions, and hardware quirks. iOS is better but still requires supporting multiple generations. Emulators don't catch the real bugs — you need physical device testing across every variant your dealers actually use.

Camera2 API behavior varies by manufacturer

Transcoding at scale

Every video needs to be transcoded to multiple resolutions, thumbnailed, and packaged into HLS/DASH manifests for adaptive streaming. At 50K videos/month that's $1,500-$4,500/month in AWS MediaConvert alone — before storage, CDN, or AI processing.

100K videos/month at HD = ~$4,500/mo transcoding costs

Storage and delivery costs

Video is heavy. A year of recordings across your dealer base hits petabyte scale fast. S3 Standard is $23/TB/month. Add CDN egress, lifecycle policies, tiering to Glacier, retention compliance, and geographic redundancy. Your storage bill becomes a line item your CFO asks about.

1 PB in S3 Standard = ~$23,000/month

AI that works in a service lane

Models trained on stock photos fail on real shop video. Shadows create false positives. Metallic paint reflections get flagged as damage. Dirty lenses cause missed detections. Mud gets classified as body damage. Getting from demo-accurate to production-accurate takes years and millions of labeled training examples.

A small CV team costs $1M+/year before cloud GPU costs

Compliance and security

Videos capture license plates, VINs, customer faces, and other vehicles. That's PII. SOC 2 compliance alone costs $30-150K in year one. Add data retention policies, encrypted storage, CJIS requirements for fleet/towing, and per-state privacy regulations. This is not optional if your customers are dealership groups.

SOC 2 Year 1: $30K-$150K | Annual maintenance: $20-60K
