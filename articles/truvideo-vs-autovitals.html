If you're evaluating digital inspection platforms for your dealership or service operation, AutoVitals and TruVideo are likely on your shortlist. Both platforms aim to digitize vehicle inspections, increase repair order revenue, and improve the customer experience. But they approach the problem from fundamentally different directions — and those differences matter more as your operation scales.

This comparison is designed to help you make an informed decision. We'll be straightforward about what AutoVitals does well, where TruVideo differs, and which scenarios favor each platform. We build TruVideo, so we're obviously not neutral — but we believe an honest comparison serves everyone better than a one-sided teardown.

Choosing a Digital Inspection Platform

The digital vehicle inspection (DVI) market has matured significantly over the past five years. What started as simple checklist digitization has evolved into a category that encompasses video capture, AI-powered damage detection, customer communication, and enterprise-grade data infrastructure. The right platform depends on where you are today and where you're headed.

Key questions to ask before evaluating any platform:

  • Are you a single-location independent shop, a multi-rooftop dealership group, or an enterprise with operations spanning multiple verticals?
  • Do you need inspections limited to automotive service, or do you also handle fleet, commercial vehicles, or other asset types?
  • How important is it that your inspection data stays on infrastructure you control?
  • Do you need API access to build custom integrations or embed inspection capabilities into your own applications?
  • Is AI-powered damage detection a current requirement or a future consideration?

Your answers to these questions will likely point you toward one platform over the other. Let's look at each.

AutoVitals: What It Does Well

AutoVitals has built a solid reputation in the automotive aftermarket, particularly among independent repair shops. Credit where it's due — they've been in the digital inspection space for years and have refined several aspects of the workflow.

Workflow automation. AutoVitals excels at structuring the inspection-to-approval workflow. Their system guides technicians through checklist-based inspections, automatically routes results to service advisors, and provides tools for building and presenting repair recommendations. The workflow is well-designed for the single-shop use case where the service advisor is the primary point of customer communication.

Checklist-based inspections. AutoVitals' inspection framework is built around configurable checklists with red/yellow/green condition indicators. Technicians work through the checklist, marking each item, and can attach photos to document findings. This approach is familiar, fast for simple inspections, and requires minimal training for technicians accustomed to paper multipoint forms.

Customer communication. AutoVitals provides tools for sharing inspection results with customers, including photo-based inspection reports that can be sent via text or email. The customer-facing presentation is clean and effective at building trust through visual documentation.

Shop management integration. AutoVitals integrates with several popular shop management systems in the independent aftermarket, allowing inspection data to flow into repair orders and customer records without double-entry.

For a single-location independent shop that needs a straightforward digital inspection tool focused on automotive service, AutoVitals is a competent choice. The limitations emerge as operations scale or requirements expand beyond the basic inspection-and-communicate workflow.

TruVideo: The Platform Approach

TruVideo approaches digital inspection from a different starting point. Rather than digitizing the paper inspection form, TruVideo treats video as the primary inspection medium and builds AI, data infrastructure, and enterprise capabilities around it.

Video-first capture. Every TruVideo inspection starts with video, not photos and checklists. Technicians record a narrated walk-around or under-vehicle inspection, capturing conditions in context with motion, perspective, and verbal explanation. The video is then processed by AI to extract structured findings, generate condition reports, and identify items requiring attention. This inverts the traditional workflow: instead of filling out a form and sometimes attaching a photo, the technician captures reality and the platform structures it.

AI-powered analysis. TruVideo's computer vision models, trained on more than 80 million videos, automatically detect damage, wear patterns, fluid leaks, tire condition, and component failures in inspection footage. This isn't a photo-recognition feature bolted onto a checklist tool — it's a core capability that processes video frame-by-frame to identify conditions a human might miss or that a photo wouldn't capture.

Enterprise platform. TruVideo is built as a platform, not a point solution. It includes a Video SDK for developers who want to embed inspection capabilities into their own applications, a Video Query Service for searching and analyzing inspection footage at scale, and APIs that integrate with dealership management systems, fleet management platforms, and enterprise resource planning systems.

Multi-vertical capability. TruVideo serves automotive, fleet and transportation, aviation MRO, field service, insurance, and construction. The same platform — with industry-specific AI models and workflows — handles vehicle inspections, aircraft maintenance documentation, field service proof-of-work, and insurance claims processing. For enterprise organizations that operate across multiple verticals, this eliminates the need for separate inspection tools in each division.

Video-First vs. Photo-Based Inspections

This is the most fundamental difference between the two platforms, and it affects everything downstream.

Photos capture moments. Video captures reality. A photograph of a worn brake pad shows the pad in a single state from a single angle. A video shows the technician removing the wheel, examining the rotor surface, measuring the pad thickness, and pointing out the wear indicator — in thirty seconds of footage that communicates more than ten photos with written annotations. Customers who see video inspections approve more work because they understand the context, not just the conclusion.

Photos require interpretation. Video provides evidence. When a service advisor presents a photo of a fluid leak to a customer, the customer sees a dark spot on a component they don't recognize. When they see a video showing the technician tracing the leak from its source, wiping the area clean, and demonstrating the active drip, they see proof. The revenue impact is measurable: dealerships using video-based inspections consistently report higher repair order values than those using photo-based tools.

Photos are static. Video enables AI. Computer vision models need temporal context to accurately identify many vehicle conditions. A frame showing discoloration on a metal surface could be corrosion, could be a shadow, could be grease. Five seconds of video showing the technician's light moving across that surface gives the AI model enough context to classify it accurately. TruVideo's AI analyzes video because video contains the information that photographs lack.

AI and Computer Vision Capabilities

AutoVitals does not offer AI-powered damage detection or automated finding generation. Their platform relies on the technician to identify, classify, and document every condition manually through the checklist interface. This approach works — technicians are skilled professionals — but it's entirely dependent on the individual technician's thoroughness, experience, and attention on each specific inspection.

TruVideo's AI operates as a consistent second set of eyes across every inspection. The system detects conditions including:

  • Tire wear patterns — uneven wear, cupping, sidewall damage, tread depth estimation
  • Fluid leaks — oil, coolant, transmission fluid, power steering fluid, with source identification
  • Brake condition — pad thickness estimation, rotor scoring, caliper condition
  • Suspension wear — boot tears, bushing deterioration, component play
  • Body damage — dents, scratches, paint damage, corrosion
  • Undercarriage condition — rust, impact damage, exhaust system deterioration

The AI doesn't replace the technician — it catches what even experienced technicians sometimes miss under time pressure. And because the AI processes every frame of every video, it provides a consistent baseline that doesn't vary by technician, shift, or workload.

Single-Vertical vs. Multi-Vertical

AutoVitals is built for automotive service. If your business is a single-location auto repair shop, that focus is a strength — the product is tailored to your workflow. But if your organization touches multiple asset types or industries, AutoVitals becomes one of several disconnected tools you need to manage.

TruVideo serves six verticals from a single platform:

  • Automotive — dealership service, independent repair, collision centers
  • Fleet & Transportation — DOT inspections, pre/post-trip DVIRs, maintenance documentation
  • Aviation — aircraft inspection documentation, NDT records, compliance audit trails
  • Field Service — proof of work, remote expert collaboration, job documentation
  • Insurance — claims documentation, damage assessment, fraud detection
  • Construction — site documentation, equipment inspection, progress verification

For a dealership group that also has a fleet maintenance division, or an enterprise that handles both automotive service and insurance claims, TruVideo provides a unified platform with shared infrastructure, shared AI models, and consolidated data management. The alternative — running AutoVitals for automotive, a separate tool for fleet, another for insurance — creates data silos, increases vendor management overhead, and prevents cross-vertical insights.

Data Sovereignty and BYOS Architecture

When you use AutoVitals, your inspection data — photos, checklists, customer information, vehicle records — lives on AutoVitals' infrastructure. You access it through their platform, and if you leave, your historical data goes with the relationship.

TruVideo offers BYOS (Bring Your Own Storage) architecture. Your inspection videos, metadata, and AI-generated findings can be stored on your own cloud infrastructure — your S3 bucket, your Azure blob storage, your GCS account. TruVideo processes and analyzes the data, but you own it, you control access to it, and it stays on your infrastructure regardless of your relationship with TruVideo.

This matters for several reasons:

  • Compliance. If you're subject to data residency requirements or industry regulations governing customer data, BYOS ensures your data stays where your compliance framework requires it.
  • Vendor independence. If you ever change platforms, your data is already on infrastructure you control. There's no migration risk, no data export negotiation, no gaps in your historical records.
  • Enterprise IT requirements. Large dealership groups and enterprise organizations typically have IT governance policies about where customer data lives. BYOS architecture aligns with those policies rather than forcing exceptions.

Making the Right Choice

AutoVitals may be the better fit if:

  • You operate a single independent repair shop focused exclusively on automotive service
  • You want a checklist-based inspection workflow that closely mirrors your existing paper process
  • You don't need AI-powered analysis or video-based inspections
  • API access and custom integrations aren't requirements
  • Data sovereignty and BYOS architecture aren't priorities for your operation

TruVideo is the better fit if:

  • You're a multi-rooftop dealership group or enterprise organization
  • You want video-first inspections that increase customer trust and repair order revenue
  • AI-powered damage detection is important for consistency and thoroughness
  • You need a platform that works across automotive and other verticals
  • You require API access to build custom integrations or embed inspection into your own tools
  • Data sovereignty matters — you want your inspection data on your infrastructure
  • You're building for scale and need a platform that grows with your organization

The distinction isn't good vs. bad. It's point solution vs. platform. If you know your needs will stay narrow, a point solution works. If you're building for where your business is headed, the platform play makes more sense.
